One-on-one Tutoring/Lab Assisting

While taking the CS 370 course, Intro to Teaching Computer Science, I was assigned weekly fieldwork and tutored three students per week in one-hour sessions. We went over any concepts or questions that they felt they needed reinforcement on, and I would curate problems to go over with them that targeted these concepts. In addition, I went to weekly lab sections and helped students with their programming assignments.

Computer Science Mentors: Small Group Tutoring

Through Computer Science Mentors (CSM), I facilitated a weekly small group section of 3-5 students. This class could be taken as a 1-unit class alongside the introductory programming course at UC Berkeley: CS 61A. Each week, I reviewed and strengthened the core of the topics covered in CS 61A by going over fundamentals and working through worksheets.

Tutor: Office Hours, Grading

As a Tutor for CS 61A through the UC Berkeley Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department, I assisted office hours, homework parties, and project parties. There, I helped students with the questions they brought; these could range from conceptual questions, to debugging and confusion on projects, labs, or homeworks. I also helped grade the composition of projects, as well the midterms and final.
